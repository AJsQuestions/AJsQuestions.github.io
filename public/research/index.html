Research

I’m interested in how learning, beliefs, and markets co‑evolve.

Rather than treating machine learning as a black box,
I use it as a structured belief generator inside economic models.

Automating Black–Litterman Beliefs with Machine Learning

Core idea:
Replace subjective investor views in Black–Litterman with ML‑generated beliefs.

Why it matters:

  • Bridges Bayesian portfolio theory and modern prediction
  • Preserves economic structure while allowing data‑driven views
  • Produces portfolios that outperform naïve benchmarks without degenerating into pure MVO

Methods:
Random Forests, Gradient Boosting, Bayesian updating, constrained mean–variance optimization.

Status:
Working paper (2024).

Illiquidity, Volatility Smoothing, and Portfolio Risk

Joint work during my time at Harvard Business School.

We study how illiquid assets distort observed volatility and expected returns,
and how classical portfolio rules fail quietly when liquidity assumptions break.

This project blends simulation, econometrics, and institutional realism.

Real Estate Pricing and Presale Risk (India)

This project was conducted during my time at
the Indian Institute of Management Bangalore (Oct 2024 – Jan 2025).

We study structural models of presale housing markets,
with the goal of quantifying risk premia embedded in presale prices
and understanding how information asymmetry shapes urban real estate markets.

Research philosophy

I care less about fancy models
and more about models that fail informatively.

If a method breaks, I want to know where, why, and what that teaches us.
