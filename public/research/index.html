I’m interested in how learning, beliefs, and markets co‑evolve. Rather than treating machine learning as a black box, I use it as a structured belief generator inside economic models.

Automating Black–Litterman Beliefs with Machine Learning

Replace subjective investor views in Black–Litterman with ML‑generated beliefs.

  • Bridges Bayesian portfolio theory and modern prediction
  • Preserves economic structure while allowing data‑driven views
  • Produces portfolios that outperform naïve benchmarks

Working paper (2024)

Illiquidity, Volatility Smoothing, and Portfolio Risk

Joint work during my time at Harvard Business School. We study how illiquid assets distort observed volatility and expected returns, and how classical portfolio rules fail quietly when liquidity assumptions break.

Real Estate Pricing and Presale Risk

Conducted at IIM Bangalore (2024–2025). Structural models of presale housing markets, quantifying risk premia and understanding how information asymmetry shapes urban real estate.

Philosophy

I care less about fancy models and more about models that fail informatively. If a method breaks, I want to know where, why, and what that teaches us.
